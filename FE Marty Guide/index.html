Character Overview

Marty is one of the worst, if not the worst, unit in Fire Emblem History. He's a huge meme in the community due to his base stats being straight-up laughable, and the fact that he's a, interestingly enough, playable brigand/ mountain thief. He joins in Chapter 1 during Turn 3, but is recruited again in Chapter 8 by having Orsin or Halvan speak to him.

Being a brigand, there was never any doubt that Marty was going to be a terrible unit. With his terrible base stats and even more terrible growths, it's obvious that Marty just isn't a great unit to begin with. However, he does excel in HP and CON growths, and has a somewhat decent DEF base stat and growth.

Most players use Marty as a capture unit, a unit to keep captured enemy units and as fodder to soak up damage. Marty can excel with appropriate scroll and arena abuse, but that is a lot of investment for a unit that's not worth it.

Thracia 776 gives you many tools to take a unit like Marty from the ground up. Despite being a huge meme in the community, Marty can has a fighting chance of being decently useful with scroll/arena abuse, skill manuals, and weapons such as the Brave Axe.

Base Stats and Growths

Level 1 Brigand HP STR MAG SKL SPD LCK DEF CON MOV
Base Stats 32 9 0 0 0 6 6 15 6
Growth Rates 90% 15% 5% 10% 15% 50% 40% 75% 2%

There it is in all of its glory. Marty's base stats! Yes, they're awful. A whopping 0 in MAG/SKL/SPD means that pitting him against a mage is a death sentence, and he'll struggle to hit literally anything. To add insult to injury, he is equipped with a Steel Axe, a super heavy weapon that would further harm his Attack Speed if his CON wasn't so high. Speaking of which, his CON is amazing! Although this makes Marty almost impossible to rescue, Marty's high CON lets him be able to capture lots of enemy units, and he's hardly weighed down by even the heaviest of weapons.

Marty has an immensely high HP and CON growth rate, at 90% and 75%. He'll gain HP and CON no problem, and even LCK, surprisingly. DEF may also increase a lot if you're lucky. However, every other stat is awful, with each one having less than a 20% chance of leveling up in any given level-up. Even Marty's base 9 STR is surprisingly low for a brigand, with a pitiful 15% growth rate. That's... so bad. So... don't expect Marty to go anywhere with these stats if you're not arena/scroll abusing. Marty has clear strengths and weaknesses. He is basically a damage sponge that has the ability to capture almost anything that moves, but is simply unable to because of his rotten SKL/SPD. At least... that's the case in the early game.

Other Stats

Marty's other stats have nothing of interest besides his promotion gains. He has no leadership/movement stars, a very low 1 PCC, no skills whatsoever, and he begins with a low Axe rank of D. Wonderful. But! He does have a really good Warrior promotion, one that is almost as good as the Sage promotion.

It offers a whopping +5 to SPD, a helpful +3 to STR and CON, +5 to SKL, and other helpful weapon ranks. Although this doesn't make Marty into a combat god, it increases his combat potential immensely. If you scroll abused him to get him to promotion, then he likely gained a few level-ups in the important stats, and this promotion will only help him further. However, even if Marty's stats end up getting close to capping, he'll still struggle due to a low PCC and lack of skills.

Character Tips

Marty can work surprisingly well in the Manster Escape Sequence. If you plan to use Marty long term and don't want to wait to abuse him in the arena, then give him the Speed Ring and the Ced scroll you get from Ced in Chapter 4x. The Odo scroll is also made available very early in the game, at Chapter 6. With these two, Marty could earn some valuable level-ups in SKL/SPD. Leveling his MAG won't be too big of a deal since Marty can get so much HP due to his 90% growth that he'll most likely soak up magic damage better than any sage ever could. The only real problem now is his horrendous STR, and only the Njörun scroll offers a significant boost in STR at 30%, but this scroll is obtainable only until you reach Chapter 18, really late in the game. Until then, pair the Hoðr and Nál scrolls on Marty, as they both will offer a 20% increase to STR.

As for skill manuals, there aren't any notable ones that could drastically improve Marty's performance. The Paragon manual could be used on Marty if you're really struggling with netting him some EXP, and there are others such as Wrath that improve his enemy phase potential, and Sol that increase his survivability.

Even with all of this scroll abuse, Marty could still fall short of important stat level-ups, and he may still fall short of other units because of his low PCC and lack of skills. Regardless, Marty is meant to be a weak character, a meme of the community, so if you followed this guide and made Marty a reliable combat unit, then I applaud you, good sir!
