Character Overview

Safy is your first staff bot, first recruited in Chapter 3, and later recruited in Chapter 7 after the Manster Escape Sequence. Safy is a great introduction to staff usage in Thracia 776 and how absurdly broken they are. Although Safy is pretty basic as a staff user, even with a PRF, she is still valuable, and it's quite easy to get her to A rank Staves quickly.

Her combat potential is practically non-existent, though. Safy gains the ability to use Light tomes upon promotion, but an E rank makes it exceptionally difficult for her to get anywhere. Not to mention her defensive stats are really poor. So, it's best to limit her utility to staff usage.

Safy joins with a high enough Staff rank to easily heal your wounded units with Heal and Physic, use Torch staves, Restore, and a variety of other powerful staves to make sure you always have the upper hand in player phase. As mentioned earlier, Safy also has a PRF that allows her to fix near broken weapons so that you'll always have access to either powerful staves or powerful weapons.

Safy is the bread and butter for a good staff user. Her combat potential and defensive stats are big weaknesses, but she's never needed for combat, and chances are, she might not even require a promotion.

Base Stats and Growths

Like any early game staff user, Safy's stats are overall pretty bad. A Base 0 DEF, 6 SPD, and 6 MAG would be a bad omen for any other unit, but Safy doesn't need great stats to be useful. Only HP, MAG, and SKL are useful to Safy, as she needs high HP to not be fatigued as often, MAG so that she can use status staves against endgame enemies, and SKL so that she can reliably hit her staff uses. Remember, Safy will never enter combat, and if she does, she's pretty much a goner. Since combat stats are irrelevant, we can look at Safy's decent 6 MAG/SKL, and low base 14 HP.

Safy's growth rates are pretty decent if you ignore her DEF/CON/MOV. She's blessed with an unusually high STR growth rate at 30%, and her HP/MAG/SKL growth rates are also pretty commendable. She won't be a stats monster, not without significant investment, but Safy is good enough as long as you get her to that 10 SKL benchmark, so that she doesn't miss, and use her frequently to level up her staff rank.

Other Stats

Safy only needs a couple staff uses to get to B rank Staves. Once she achieves that, she can promote to instantly attain A rank staves, as her High Priest promotion grants an extra Staff rank. Safy starts to really shine once she achieves A rank staves and a high enough MAG. If you can get your hands on a few rare status staves in the early-mid game, then Safy can easily make your life much easier.

Her promotion is pretty lackluster, though. She gets a +1 to the stats mentioned above, but it's not enough to fix her horrid combat stats. To add insult to injury, Safy only gains the ability to use Light tomes upon promotion, and even then, it's only at E rank. This is the game's way of telling you that you should not try to invest in a combat Safy. There's just really no point to it. A total lack of skills doesn't help her case. Oh, also, she only has a PCC of 1. Yeah. Just don't make Safy engage in combat.

Besides her lackluster promotion, Safy's other stats are also pretty unremarkable. No movement or leadership stars, and a dreadful PCC of 1. Don't take this the wrong way, Safy is still a solid unit, but it's clear where her usefulness lies.

PRF's and Support Bonuses

Name Range Uses Weight EXP Gain Effects
Hammerne 1 5 6 30 Repairs an adjacent ally's weapon to full durability

Hammerne, or the "Repair" staff, is an awesome item that refills the durability of any weapon/staff. I highly advise that you do not hoard this staff, since there's no reason not to use it, but be mindful about its uses. You're going to want to use this staff on other PRF's, status staves, master weapons, brave weapons, and the like. Things like Warp, the Light Brand, Mareeta's Sword, Earth Sword, Silence, Sleep, etc. If you find yourself using a certain weapon/staff a bit too much, and it's about to run out of uses, consider a Hammerne use. Safy's existence makes your gameplay a lot less frustrating.

Supports: Shiva, Lifis, Tina

Supported by: Leif, Tina

Character Tips

The stats you should focus on Safy are the ones that will increase her usefulness. First, her HP. A higher HP means she can perform more actions without being fatigued, although this is not necessary if you choose not to bring her to endgame. Regardless, the Hoðr and Nál scrolls are your best bet when trying to increase her HP. As for Safy's SKL, her most important stat when you recruit her, my advice is to give her the Odo and never take it off until she has hit 10 SKL. It offers the most significant boost to SKL, and it is not much use to anyone else, so make sure that this scroll is reserved for all of your staff users, including Safy. Safy's MAG has a good chance of capping naturally after leveling and promoting. If this is not the case for you because of RNG screwing, the Heim scroll can easily help her cap the MAG stat, guaranteed.

On the battlefield, keep Safy out of all combat, that goes without saying. Her Staff rank and PRF is more than enough to be a godsend to your team in many chapters of the game. Whether you need to Warp a unit to skip a chapter, Rescue one out of a sticky situation, Restore a status, or Physic someone from far away, Safy's your girl. Her existence is meant to make your life easier.
